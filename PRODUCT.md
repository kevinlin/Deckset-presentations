# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Peers and evaluators: colleagues, hiring managers, and conference organizers who visit to judge the quality of Kevin's talks and workshops. They arrive with limited time and a professional eye, deciding whether the work merits a follow-up.

## Product Purpose

A static website generated from Deckset-compatible Markdown: a homepage listing every deck, plus an interactive slide viewer per presentation. It puts Kevin's real talks (Docker/Kubernetes, EKS, Auth0 with Terraform, RAG, technical messaging) in front of people deciding whether to engage him. Success: a visitor leaves impressed by the presentation craft and by the generator behind it.

## Positioning

A working portfolio of serious technical talks; the archive itself is the evidence.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: open a deck. Secondary: browse the full deck list without committing to one.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "I should get this person in front of my team."
- Belief ladder: (1) these are real talks on serious, current topics; (2) there is range here (cloud, auth, RAG, containers, communication); (3) opening a deck will be worth two minutes.
- Proof on hand: the decks themselves, seven full presentations in this repo. No testimonials or venue list yet; add files under `.impeccable/assets/proof/` if that changes.

## Brand Personality

Crisp, technical, precise. Closest reference feel: Framer and Webflow, where the site's own construction shows off the craft. Confidence comes from restraint and sharp detail.

## Anti-references

- Corporate slideware: SharePoint-era deck archives, PowerPoint-export chrome.
- Generic SaaS landing: hero, three feature cards, testimonial strip.
- Bare docs site: plain utilitarian lists that undersell the work.

## Design Principles

1. The archive is the audition. Every surface (homepage, deck chrome, slide rendering) is evidence of craft.
2. Substance first. Deck titles and topics carry the first impression; polish frames the work.
3. Shortest path into a deck. Opening a presentation is the conversion; nothing stands between landing and slides.
4. Precision as personality. Every detail is deliberate; drama comes from motion and staging.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text contrast at or above 4.5:1, full keyboard navigation in the slide viewer, and reduced-motion alternatives for every animation.
